**Funchal**   
Monte Palace Tropical Gardens \- 

* located in the hills above Funchal and feature a large collection of exotic plants, various sculptures and walkways.  
* entrance fee: 15 euros.  
* To get there, we can take a [cable car](https://madeiracablecar.com/en/): 14.5 euros one-way or 20 euros 2-way.

Funchal Farmers Market (Mercado dos Lavradores)

* selling fresh produce, flowers, spices and seafood

Funchal Old Town (Zona Velha)

* make sure to stroll along Rua de Santa Maria  
* Other famous attractions in the Old Town are the 15th-century Corpo Santo Chapel, the yellow São Tiago Fort and Madeira Story Centre museum.

PR1: Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo hike

* \~45 minutes drive from Funchal.  
* It is recommended to **see the sunrise from the *Miradouro do Juncal* viewpoint**.  
* A pretty challenging and steep 12 km out-and-back trail.  
* During the hike, you’ll need to go through several tunnels carved into the mountains, so make sure to **bring a head torch or a phone flashlight**  
* **Only the first 1.2 km are open** \- from Pico do Arieiro to Pedra Rija viewpoint, which includes the ‘Stairway to Heaven’. It’s absolutely beautiful and still worth doing.  
* It is possible to access the other end of the trail and visit Pico Ruivo via the PR 1.2 trail.

Northwestern coast:

* São Vicente \- A picturesque little town, simply stroll around the town’s historic center and the oceanfront area.  
* Seixal  
  * Before entering the town, stop at *Miradouro do Véu da Noiva*, a famous viewpoint where you can admire the Bridal Veil Waterfall and the towering cliffs around it.  
  * head to Seixal Beach which is one of the few naturally sandy beaches on the island.  
  * Seixal Natural Pools are surrounded by volcanic formations and filled with crystal clear water.  
  * As you leave Seixal and continue driving northwest along the coast, visit also *Miradouro da Eira da Achada* and *Miradouro Ilheus da Ribeira da Janela* viewpoints.  
* Porto Moniz  
  * a small seaside town known for its two sets of natural pools – one is more developed and kid-friendly and the other one is a bit more ‘rough’.  
  * If we don’t have time, skip it.  
* Achadas da Cruz Cable Car  
  * you’ll be transported down an almost vertical cliff and arrive at a beautiful isolated stretch of coastline. It’s an exciting 5-minute ride and costs just 5 euros for a round-trip.  
  * Down by the shore, there’s a tiny traditional village, a pebble beach and a walking trail with amazing views of the cliffs towering above you.

Levada walks and Fanal Forest

* PR6: Levada das 25 Fontes walk & Risco Waterfall  
  * \*\* maybe do a different levada trail  
  * An easy levada walk featuring a scenic forest trail and several waterfalls. The trail leads to a small lagoon surrounded by tall rock walls with dozens of tiny streams trickling down them.  
  * One of the most popular levada walks in Madeira, better to get there early.  
  * To get to the 100m tall Risco Waterfall, walk the same way back from the 25 Fontes lagoon and turn left at the fork about halfway through the trail. To visit both of these waterfalls, it’s an 11 km round-trip hike and should take you 3-4 hours.  
* Fanal Forest  
  * Auto Fanal in Google Maps.  
  * It really looks like an enchanted forest and makes you feel like you’ve stepped into a fairytale.  
  * It’s crazy to think that most of these trees are more than 600 years old and have been growing here since before Madeira was even discovered\!

The ‘tail’ of Madeira

* PR8: Ponta de São Lourenço  
  * This long narrow peninsula, which is also known as the “tail of Madeira”, features spectacular volcanic cliffs, rock formations and sweeping views of the Atlantic Ocean.  
  * easy 8 km round-trip hike, about 3 hours.  
  * Near the end of the trail, there’s also a small cafe where you can get snacks or a cold drink.  
  * Recommend hiking a bit further than the cafe to the *Miradouro Ponta do Furado* viewpoint for incredible views of the Ilhéu da Cevada islet.  
* Prainha Beach (Prainha do Caniçal)  
  * A short drive from Ponta de São Lourenço.  
  * Soft volcanic sand and turquoise water full of colorful fish.

Northeastern:

* PR9: Levada do Caldeirão Verde  
  * 12 km easy flat hike, \~4 hours, need head torch/ flashlight.  
  * The trail leads you through a lush Laurisilva forest, past several small waterfalls and offers magnificent views of the green valleys and mountains of Madeira.  
  * At the end of the hike, you’ll arrive at the mysterious Caldeirão Verde (aka the “green cauldron”), where you’ll see a 110m tall waterfall cascading down green mossy cliffs.  
* Santana  
  * With triangle-shaped thatched roofs and colorful facades, these tiny houses look like they belong in a fairytale.  
  * The best place to see them is in the town center, next to the Town Hall (Casas típicas de Santana in Google Maps).  
* Miradouro do Guindaste \- offers some mesmerizing views of the ocean and the massive cliffs along Madeira’s northeastern coast.

Pico Grande

* An out-and-back 15 km trail (1km elevation gain)  
* A great alternative to the PR1 which is mainly closed.  
* Explanation how to get there \- [https://www.journeyera.com/pico-grande-madeira/](https://www.journeyera.com/pico-grande-madeira/) 

Levada Do Moinho & Levada Nova 

* An round-trip 9 km trail (130m elevation gain)  
* Parking & Start Point: [Levada do Moinho Trailhead – Ponta do Sol](https://goo.gl/maps/vogtU3x2bnYxG2wGA)  
* Route Map: [WalkMe Madeira – Levada Nova](https://www.walkmeguide.com/en/madeira/trails/20-levada-nova/)  
* The path is less than a metre wide in places and is quite exposed, with steep drops into the valley below.

Bica Da Cana

* Out-and-back 1.5km trail (elevation gain 40m)  
* Best for sunrise.  
* Just make sure to get there early, as parking is limited and this viewpoint is popular with photographers and sunrise chasers.

Cabo Girão

* Cabo Girão Skywalk is a viewpoint, 580 meters above sea level that offers on of the best views in Madeira. The best of all? You’ll be walking on a glass floor so you can see everything that’s going on underneath your feet.  
* Opening hours: 8:00-21:00, costs 5 euros.  
* **Fajas de Cabo Girão** is a small piece of land where farmers cultivate their crops. It’s visible from the viewpoint and **you can also visit it by a cable car**. It’s a fascinating ride going down a 500+ meters cliff.
